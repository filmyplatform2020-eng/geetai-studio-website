## SECTION 5 — SENTIMENT QUICK-READ CHEAT SHEET
When scanning a review fast, look for these signal words to gauge true sentiment (sometimes star rating doesn't match tone):
- **Hidden positive in 3-star:** "good but...", "decent, would be great if...", "works fine, just wish..."
- **Hidden frustration in 4-star:** "mostly good", "no major complaints", "does the job" (lukewarm language = room to win them over)
- **Genuine anger (handle with care):** ALL CAPS, exclamation chains, "never again", "waste of money" — these need fastest response + most personal tone

## 8 RULES FOR REPUTATION MANAGEMENT THAT BUILDS TRUST
1. Reply to EVERY review — positive and negative. Silence on bad reviews looks worse than the review itself.
2. Respond within 24-48 hours — speed signals you care
3. Always personalize — use their name, reference specifics from their review
4. Never argue publicly — take disputes to DM/email immediately
5. Thank reviewers genuinely — even short positive reviews deserve a real reply, not a copy-paste
6. Turn negative reviews into proof — a well-handled bad review often impresses future customers MORE than a good one
7. Ask for reviews at the right moment — right after a positive interaction, not randomly
8. Track patterns — if the same complaint appears 3+ times, it's not a one-off, it's a fixable system issue
