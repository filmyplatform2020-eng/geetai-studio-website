## SECTION 1 — THE STAR ANSWER FRAMEWORK (for behavioral questions)
- **Situation**: Brief context — what was happening, when, where?
- **Task**: What were you responsible for or trying to achieve?
- **Action**: What specifically did YOU do? (the most important part — be concrete, use "I" not "we")
- **Result**: What happened? Quantify where possible — numbers make answers memorable.

**Practice prompt structure:** "Tell me about a time you [situation type]" → map your real experience to STAR before the interview, for each common theme (conflict, failure, leadership, deadline pressure, etc.)

## SECTION 3 — STAGE-BY-STAGE SCRIPTS

### Stage 1 — Screening/Recruiter Call
**Opening framing:** "Thanks for reaching out — I'd love to learn more about the role and share a bit about my background too."
**Salary question handling:** "I'm looking for a role that's a strong overall fit — I'm open to discussing compensation once we've explored whether this is the right match for both sides. What range does this role typically fall in?"

### Stage 2 — First-Round/Hiring Manager Interview
**Opening self-introduction template:** "I'm currently [role/context], and over the past [timeframe] I've focused on [relevant area]. What drew me to this role is [specific, genuine reason tied to the company/role]."

### Stage 3 — Final-Round/Panel Interview
**Handling multiple interviewers:** Address the person who asked the question directly, but briefly include others in your eye contact/closing line — shows awareness of the group dynamic.

### Stage 4 — Offer Negotiation
**Negotiation opener:** "Thank you so much for the offer — I'm genuinely excited about this opportunity. Based on [market research/your experience level], I was hoping we could explore [specific ask — salary/benefits/start date] — is there flexibility there?"
**Graceful acceptance:** "Thank you — I'm excited to accept and get started. Could we confirm [start date/next steps] so I can plan accordingly?"
**Graceful decline:** "I really appreciate the offer and enjoyed learning about [Company] — after careful consideration, I've decided to pursue a different opportunity that's a closer fit for [reason, brief and respectful]. Thank you again for your time."

## 6 RULES FOR INTERVIEWS THAT LEAD TO OFFERS
1. Prepare stories, not just facts — interviewers remember specific stories far longer than generic claims
2. Practice OUT LOUD — thinking through an answer is different from saying it smoothly under pressure
3. Research the company/role deeply — generic answers are the #1 reason strong candidates don't advance
4. Always have questions ready — it signals genuine interest and that you're evaluating fit too
5. Send a thank-you note within 24 hours — small gesture, disproportionately positive impact
6. Treat every stage as a two-way conversation — confidence (not arrogance) comes from knowing you're assessing fit too
