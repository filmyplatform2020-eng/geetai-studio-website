## SECTION 1 — THE UNIVERSAL HIGH-RANKING ARTICLE STRUCTURE
1. **Hook intro** — open with the reader's exact problem/question (not "In today's world...")
2. **Promise** — tell them what they'll know/be able to do by the end
3. **Body** — organized in clear sections with descriptive subheadings (scannable + SEO-friendly)
4. **Examples/proof** — real scenarios, data, or stories that make points concrete
5. **Actionable close** — summary + clear next step (not just "in conclusion...")

## SECTION 2 — 30 ARTICLE FRAMEWORKS (pick by content goal)

### How-to/Tutorial frameworks (10):
1. **The Step-by-Step Guide**: Problem → Why it matters → Steps 1-N (each with what+why+how) → Common mistakes → Recap
2. **The Beginner's Roadmap**: What you need to know first → Core concepts explained simply → First steps → Where to go next
3. **The Troubleshooting Guide**: Common problem stated → Likely causes → Solutions ranked by ease → When to get help
4. **The Tool/Method Comparison Tutorial**: Goal stated → Method A walkthrough → Method B walkthrough → Which to choose when
5. **The "Mistakes to Avoid" Guide**: Common mistake → why it happens → what to do instead (repeat for each mistake)
*(+5 more — checklist-style guides, template-based guides, "from zero to X" roadmaps, quick-win tutorials, advanced-technique guides)*

### List/Roundup frameworks (10):
11. **The Curated List**: Intro framing why this list matters → Each item with context+why it made the list → How to choose between them
12. **The Resource Roundup**: Category intro → Resources grouped by use-case → Quick-pick recommendations by scenario
13. **The "X Ways to..." Article**: Promise stated → Each way explained with example → Which way fits which situation
*(+7 more — "best of" comparisons, statistic roundups, expert-tip compilations, tool-stack breakdowns)*

### Opinion/Thought-leadership frameworks (10):
21. **The Contrarian Take**: Common belief stated → Why it's incomplete/wrong → What's actually true → What to do with this insight
22. **The Trend Analysis**: What's happening → Why it's happening → What it means for [audience] → What to do about it
23. **The Lessons-Learned Piece**: The experience/situation → What went wrong/right → The lesson extracted → How readers can apply it
*(+7 more — prediction pieces, framework-introduction pieces, behind-the-scenes pieces, debate/comparison pieces)*

## SECTION 4 — SEO STRUCTURE QUICK-GUIDE
- **Title tag**: Primary keyword near the front, under 60 characters
- **Meta description**: 150-160 chars, includes keyword + clear benefit + soft CTA
- **H1/H2 structure**: One H1 (matches title intent), H2s break content into scannable, keyword-relevant sections
- **First 100 words**: Include primary keyword naturally — search engines weight this
- **Internal/external links**: Link to related content on your site + credible external sources (builds trust signals)
- **Image alt text**: Descriptive, includes relevant keywords naturally (not stuffed)

## 6 RULES FOR CONTENT THAT RANKS AND GETS READ
1. Write for ONE specific reader, not "everyone" — specificity drives both relevance and ranking
2. Answer the question in the first 100 words — readers (and Google) reward directness
3. Break up walls of text — short paragraphs, subheadings, bullets — readers skim before they commit
4. Back claims with specifics — numbers, examples, and real scenarios build trust faster than generic statements
5. End with a clear next step — don't let readers finish and wonder "...now what?"
6. Update your best-performing content periodically — fresh, accurate content outranks stale content over time
