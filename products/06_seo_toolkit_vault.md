## SECTION 1 — THE 40-POINT SEO AUDIT CHECKLIST

### A. Technical Foundation (10 checks)
☐ Site loads in under 3 seconds (test: PageSpeed Insights)
☐ Mobile-friendly / responsive on all major screen sizes
☐ HTTPS enabled (secure padlock in browser)
☐ XML sitemap exists and is submitted to Google Search Console
☐ Robots.txt isn't accidentally blocking important pages
☐ No broken links (404s) on key pages
☐ Clean, readable URL structure (no long strings of parameters)
☐ Proper redirects (301) for any moved/removed pages
☐ Images compressed and using descriptive file names + alt text
☐ Core Web Vitals pass (LCP, FID/INP, CLS — check Search Console report)

### B. On-Page Optimization (10 checks)
☐ Each page targets one primary keyword (no cannibalization between pages)
☐ Title tags under 60 characters, include primary keyword near the front
☐ Meta descriptions under 160 characters, include keyword + compelling reason to click
☐ One H1 per page, matches search intent
☐ H2-H3 structure organizes content logically (not just for looks — helps both readers and search engines)
☐ Primary keyword appears naturally in first 100 words
☐ Internal links connect related pages (helps both users and crawlers)
☐ External links to credible sources where relevant (builds trust signals)
☐ Content matches search intent (informational vs transactional vs navigational)
☐ Content length matches what's currently ranking for that keyword (check top 5 results)

### C. Content Quality Signals (10 checks)
☐ Content answers the searcher's question completely — no thin/filler content
☐ Original insights/data/examples — not just rehashed competitor content
☐ Clear structure — scannable with headers, bullet points, short paragraphs
☐ Up-to-date information (dates, stats, examples refreshed regularly)
☐ Author credibility signals present (bio, credentials, experience) — especially for YMYL topics
☐ No keyword stuffing — reads naturally for humans first
☐ Includes relevant visuals (images/charts/screenshots) that add value
☐ Clear call-to-action aligned with the content's purpose
☐ Content is genuinely better than what's currently ranking (not just "more of the same")
☐ Internal linking points readers toward logical next steps

### D. Off-Page & Authority Signals (10 checks)
☐ Google Business Profile claimed and optimized (for local businesses)
☐ Backlink profile reviewed — no spammy/toxic links dragging down authority
☐ Social profiles linked and active (signals legitimacy)
☐ Listed in relevant industry directories
☐ Brand mentioned/cited by other credible sites in the niche
☐ Customer reviews present and being actively managed
☐ Consistent NAP (Name, Address, Phone) across all listings (local SEO)
☐ Guest content or collaborations with relevant sites in the niche
☐ Shareable assets exist (guides, tools, original research) that naturally attract links
☐ Competitor backlink gaps identified and being pursued

## SECTION 2 — KEYWORD RESEARCH FRAMEWORK (5-step process)

**Step 1 — Seed list:** Brainstorm 10-15 broad terms describing what you offer (e.g., "business automation," "social media management")

**Step 2 — Expand with intent variations:** For each seed, generate variations across 4 intent types:
- Informational: "how to [do X]", "what is [X]"
- Comparison: "[X] vs [Y]", "best [X] for [use case]"
- Transactional: "[X] pricing", "buy [X]", "[X] near me"
- Navigational: "[brand] reviews", "[brand] alternative"

**Step 3 — Check difficulty vs opportunity:** Prioritize keywords where: search volume is meaningful BUT competition isn't dominated by huge authority sites — these are your realistic wins early on

**Step 4 — Map keywords to funnel stage:** Informational → top-of-funnel content (blog/guides); Comparison → mid-funnel (comparison pages); Transactional → bottom-funnel (landing/product pages)

**Step 5 — Cluster and prioritize:** Group related keywords into topic clusters (one pillar page + several supporting pages) rather than treating each keyword as a standalone target — this is how modern search engines reward topical authority

## QUICK-WIN PRIORITIZATION GUIDE
When time/resources are limited, fix issues in this order for fastest impact:
1. **Technical blockers first** (broken site = nothing else matters — fix crawlability/speed/mobile issues)
2. **Title tags + meta descriptions** (quick edits, can lift click-through rate within days)
3. **Internal linking** (cheap to do, helps both users and search engines understand your site)
4. **Content gaps on highest-intent pages** (improve pages closest to conversion first)
5. **New content for underserved keyword clusters** (longer-term compounding growth)
