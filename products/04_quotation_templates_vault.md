## UNIVERSAL QUOTATION STRUCTURE
1. Header — Your business name/logo, quote number, date, validity period
2. Client details — Name, company, contact
3. Itemized breakdown — Item/service | Description | Qty | Rate | Amount
4. Subtotal, tax, total — clearly separated
5. Terms — Payment terms, validity, what happens after acceptance
6. Notes — Any clarifications, assumptions made
7. Acceptance line — Signature/confirmation field + next-step CTA

## PRICING PSYCHOLOGY — 6 TACTICS THAT INCREASE ACCEPTANCE
1. **Anchor with 3 options** — present Basic/Standard/Premium; most clients pick the middle
2. **Itemize, don't lump** — breaking cost into line items feels more transparent and justified than one big number
3. **Frame as investment, not cost** — "Investment: ₹X" reads differently than "Price: ₹X"
4. **Add urgency naturally** — "valid for 7 days" or "current rates — increasing next month" prompts faster decisions
5. **Bundle strategically** — "Save ₹X when you choose the [bundle]" makes the higher tier feel like the smart choice
6. **Round numbers build trust in B2C, precise numbers build trust in B2B** — ₹999 for consumer-facing, ₹47,250 for itemized B2B work

4. "Hi [Name], no pressure at all — just checking if you need any more information to move forward, or if timing has shifted on your end."
5. "Hi [Name], should I close this quote for now, or would it help to revisit it in [timeframe]? Either way, happy to stay in touch."
