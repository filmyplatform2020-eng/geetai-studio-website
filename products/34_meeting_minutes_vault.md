## SECTION 1 — THE UNIVERSAL MEETING MINUTES TEMPLATE

**Header block:**
"Meeting: [Topic] | Date: [Date] | Attendees: [Names] | Next meeting: [Date/TBD]"

**Body — 4 sections every set of minutes needs:**
1. **Key decisions made** — bullet list, stated plainly ("Decided: [decision]")
2. **Action items** — Who + What + By when (table format works best)
3. **Open questions/unresolved items** — things that need follow-up before next meeting
4. **Notes/context** — brief supporting details (only what's needed to understand the decisions)

## SECTION 2 — 15 MEETING-TYPE SPECIFIC FORMATS

**Format 1 — Client/Project Status Meeting**
- Project status snapshot (on track / at risk / blocked)
- Decisions made this session
- Action items (owner + deadline)
- Risks/blockers flagged + owner for resolving
- Next check-in date

**Format 2 — Internal Team Standup/Sync**
- What's done since last sync
- What's in progress
- What's blocked + who can help unblock
- Decisions/announcements

**Format 3 — Sales/Discovery Call Summary**
- Prospect's stated needs/goals
- Objections raised + how addressed
- Next step agreed (with date)
- Internal notes (deal stage, follow-up priority)

**Format 4 — Strategy/Planning Session**
- Goals discussed
- Options considered + why final direction was chosen
- Decisions locked in
- Owners + timelines for next phase

**Format 5 — Brainstorm/Ideation Session**
- Ideas generated (grouped by theme)
- Ideas selected to pursue + why
- Next steps for top ideas
- Parking lot (good ideas, not now)

## SECTION 3 — ACTION ITEM TRACKER FORMAT (table-ready)

| Action Item | Owner | Deadline | Status | Notes |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| [Specific task] | [Name] | [Date] | Not started/In progress/Done | [Context if needed] |

**Rules for writing action items that actually get done:**
- Start with a verb: "Send," "Draft," "Confirm," "Schedule" — not vague nouns like "Follow-up"
- Always assign ONE owner — shared ownership = no ownership
- Always include a date — "soon" and "ASAP" don't count

## SECTION 4 — FOLLOW-UP EMAIL TEMPLATES (send within 24 hours)

**Template A — Internal team recap:**
"Hi team, recap from today's [meeting]: 

Key decisions: [bullets]
Action items: [name — task — by date]
Open questions: [bullets]

Let me know if I missed anything!"

**Template B — Client/external recap:**
"Hi [Name], great talking today — quick recap to keep us aligned:

What we discussed: [brief summary]
What we agreed: [decisions/next steps]
What happens next: [who does what, by when]

Looking forward to [next milestone]!"

**Template C — Decision confirmation (for high-stakes meetings):**
"Hi [Name], wanted to confirm in writing what we agreed on today: [decision, stated clearly]. Please reply to confirm we're aligned, or flag anything that needs adjusting."

## SECTION 5 — LIVE NOTE-TAKING FRAMEWORK (capture in real-time without missing key points)
Use this 3-column structure while the meeting happens:
- **Column 1 — Said**: brief notes on what was discussed (don't try to capture everything — only what matters)
- **Column 2 — Decided**: anything that was agreed/decided (mark clearly — these become your "key decisions")
- **Column 3 — Action**: anything that needs follow-up (mark with owner if mentioned, or flag "needs owner")

After the meeting: convert Column 2 → Key Decisions section, Column 3 → Action Items table.

---

## 6 RULES FOR MINUTES THAT ACTUALLY GET USED
1. Send the recap within 24 hours — the longer you wait, the less useful (and less trusted) it becomes
2. Decisions and action items are the ONLY non-negotiable sections — everything else is optional context
3. Write action items so clearly that someone who WASN'T in the meeting could execute them
4. Always name an owner — "the team will handle it" guarantees nothing happens
5. Keep it short — a 1-page summary gets read; a 5-page transcript gets ignored
6. Close the loop — start the next meeting by reviewing action items from the last one
