## SECTION 2 — 40 RESEARCH QUESTIONS (organized by what you're trying to learn)

### Understanding their problem (10):
1. "What's the most frustrating part of [task/situation] for you right now?"
2. "When was the last time [problem] happened — walk me through it?"
3. "What have you already tried to solve this? What happened?"
4. "If you could wave a magic wand, what would change about [situation]?"
5. "How much time/money does [problem] cost you currently?"
*(+5 more — root-cause digging, frequency/severity questions)*

### Understanding their decision process (10):
11. "What made you start looking for a solution to this?"
12. "What almost stopped you from buying/trying [category of solution]?"
13. "What would make you say 'yes' immediately vs. 'let me think about it'?"
14. "Who else is involved in this decision, if anyone?"
15. "What's worked well — or badly — with similar solutions you've tried?"
*(+5 more — budget signals, timeline questions, trust-building questions)*

### Understanding their world (10):
21. "Where do you usually go to learn about [topic/category]?"
22. "Who do you trust for advice on [related decisions]?"
23. "What does a typical day/week look like for you in this context?"
24. "What other tools/services do you currently use alongside this?"
25. "What's something people often get wrong about your situation?"
*(+5 more — community questions, influence-mapping questions)*

### Understanding their language (10):
31. "How would you describe [problem] to a friend in your own words?"
32. "What would you type into Google to find a solution to this?"
33. "What's the #1 outcome you're hoping for — in your words, not industry terms?"
*(+7 more — phrase-mining prompts for review/forum/comment analysis)*

## SECTION 3 — RESEARCH SOURCE MAP (where to find real customer language, free)
- **Reviews** (Amazon, G2, Trustpilot, app stores): mine for repeated phrases, complaints, "I wish it could..."
- **Reddit/forums/Quora**: search [your category] + "recommend"/"alternative"/"problem with" — raw unfiltered language
- **Social comments**: competitor posts, complaint threads — what frustrations come up repeatedly?
- **Support tickets/DMs** (if you have existing customers): the most accurate language available — use it verbatim in marketing
- **Sales call recordings/notes**: objections and triggers in real conversations

---

## SECTION 4 — SEGMENT-MAPPING FRAMEWORK
For each potential customer segment, score 1-5:
- **Pain intensity**: How badly do they feel this problem?
- **Willingness to pay**: Have they paid for solutions before?
- **Reachability**: Can you actually get in front of them affordably?
- **Fit**: Does your solution match their specific context well?

→ Total score 16-20 = primary segment (build everything around them first) | 10-15 = secondary | below 10 = deprioritize

---

## SECTION 5 — TURNING RESEARCH INTO ACTION
- **Marketing copy**: Use their EXACT phrases from research — not your internal description of the problem
- **Product priorities**: Build/fix what shows up most often in "frustrations," not what's easiest to build
- **Channel choice**: Show up where they said they "go to learn" — don't guess
- **Objection-handling**: Pre-answer the top 3 objections directly in your sales materials

---

## 6 RULES FOR RESEARCH THAT ACTUALLY CHANGES YOUR STRATEGY
1. Talk to real people, not your assumptions — 5 real conversations beat 50 guesses
2. Ask about past behavior, not hypothetical future behavior — "what did you do" beats "would you..."
3. Listen for the EXACT words they use — that's your future marketing copy, verbatim
4. Look for patterns across multiple sources — one loud opinion isn't a trend
5. Re-research periodically — your customer's world changes, and so should your understanding
6. Turn findings into ACTION immediately — research that sits in a doc helps no one
