## SECTION 1 — THE BRAND FOUNDATION FRAMEWORK (define before you write anything)

1. **Core promise** — The ONE thing your brand consistently delivers ("We help [audience] [achieve outcome] through [unique approach]")
2. **Personality** — 3-5 adjectives that describe how your brand "feels" if it were a person (e.g., "warm, direct, a little irreverent")
3. **Voice spectrum** — Where you sit on key spectrums: Formal ↔ Casual | Serious ↔ Playful | Reserved ↔ Bold | Technical ↔ Simple
4. **What you're NOT** — Equally important: explicitly defining what your brand avoids sounding/looking like
5. **Audience in one line** — Who you're speaking to, and what they care about most

## SECTION 5 — MESSAGING CONSISTENCY CHECKLIST (apply across every channel)
- [ ] Does this sound like something our brand would actually say?
- [ ] Does it speak to OUR specific audience, or could it apply to anyone?
- [ ] Is the core promise/value clear within the first few seconds/lines?
- [ ] Does the tone match the context without breaking from the core voice?
- [ ] Would someone unfamiliar with the brand understand what we stand for from this alone?

## 6 RULES FOR BRAND MESSAGING THAT STICKS
1. Specificity beats cleverness — a clear, specific promise outperforms a clever line that says nothing concrete
2. Consistency compounds — a slightly-less-perfect voice used consistently beats a perfect voice used inconsistently
3. Write for your actual audience's ear — not for what sounds impressive to you or your peers
4. Show, don't just claim — "we move fast" means less than a specific example of moving fast
5. Revisit your foundation as you grow — brand voice should evolve deliberately, not drift accidentally
6. Test your tagline/positioning out loud — if it's hard to say naturally, it'll be hard for others to remember and repeat
