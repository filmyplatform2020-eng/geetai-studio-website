## SECTION 1 — THE CUSTOMER LIFECYCLE PIPELINE (5 stages)

**Stage 1: NEW LEAD** → Goal: qualify quickly without feeling like an interrogation
**Stage 2: ENGAGED/NURTURING** → Goal: build trust, demonstrate value before asking for the sale
**Stage 3: ACTIVE NEGOTIATION** → Goal: address concerns, create urgency without pressure
**Stage 4: CUSTOMER (won)** → Goal: onboard smoothly, set up for long-term relationship
**Stage 5: RETENTION/EXPANSION** → Goal: keep them engaged, identify upsell/referral opportunities

## SECTION 2 — STAGE-BY-STAGE SCRIPTS & ACTIONS

### STAGE 1 — NEW LEAD (Qualification)
**Action checklist:** Log source → send intro message within 1 hour → ask 2-3 qualifying questions → tag as hot/warm/cold

**Qualification script:**
"Hi [Name], thanks for your interest in [product/service]! To point you toward the right solution — could you share a bit about [key qualifying question relevant to your offer]?"

**Tagging criteria:**
- 🔥 Hot: clear need + budget + timeline mentioned
- 🌤 Warm: clear need, budget/timeline unclear — needs nurturing
- ❄️ Cold: browsing/early-stage — long-term nurture list

### STAGE 2 — ENGAGED / NURTURING
**Action checklist:** Share relevant value (case study/resource/insight) → check in without pitching → identify their specific pain point clearly

**Value-first touchpoint script:**
"Hi [Name], thought of you when I saw [relevant resource/insight] — given what you mentioned about [their goal/challenge], this might be useful: [link/resource]. No agenda, just thought it'd help!"

**Pain-point discovery script:**
"Hi [Name], curious — what's the biggest challenge you're facing with [relevant area] right now? Always helps me understand how to be most useful."

### STAGE 3 — ACTIVE NEGOTIATION
**Action checklist:** Address objections directly → present options (not ultimatums) → create natural urgency → confirm next step clearly

**Objection-handling framework — "Acknowledge → Reframe → Confirm":**
- Acknowledge: "Totally understand [concern] — that's a fair thing to consider."
- Reframe: "Here's how [similar customer] thought about it: [reframe with value/proof]"
- Confirm: "Does that help address the concern, or is there something else on your mind?"

**Common objections + reframes:**
- "It's too expensive" → "I hear you — let's look at it as [cost per outcome/timeframe] rather than the upfront number. Here's what similar customers saved/gained: [proof point]"
- "I need to think about it" → "Of course — what specifically would help you feel confident moving forward? Happy to address it directly."
- "Not the right time" → "Understood — would it help to start with a smaller scope now and expand when timing's better?"

### STAGE 4 — CUSTOMER (Onboarding)
**Action checklist:** Send welcome message within 24 hours → set clear expectations → schedule first check-in → introduce support channels

**Welcome script:**
"Hi [Name], welcome aboard! 🎉 Here's what happens next: [step 1], [step 2], [step 3]. I'll personally check in on [date] to make sure everything's going smoothly. Reach out anytime at [contact]!"

**First check-in script (3-7 days in):**
"Hi [Name], wanted to check in — how's everything going so far with [product/service]? Anything I can help clarify or adjust?"

### STAGE 5 — RETENTION / EXPANSION
**Action checklist:** Schedule periodic check-ins → watch for upsell signals → ask for referrals at the right moments → flag at-risk accounts early

**Quarterly check-in script:**
"Hi [Name], it's been [X months] — wanted to check in on how things are going and see if there's anything we can do to support your next goals."

**Upsell-signal script (when they mention growth/new needs):**
"Hi [Name], based on what you mentioned about [new need/goal], it sounds like [upgrade/additional service] could be a great fit. Want me to walk you through how it would work for your situation?"

**Referral-ask script (after a positive milestone):**
"Hi [Name], so glad [result/milestone] worked out well! If you know anyone else who might benefit from [solution], I'd really appreciate the introduction — happy to return the favor anytime."

## SECTION 3 — RE-ENGAGEMENT SYSTEM (for "gone quiet" contacts)

**The 4-touch win-back sequence:**

**Touch 1 (Day 1) — Light check-in:**
"Hi [Name], hope all's well! Realized it's been a bit since we connected — anything I can help with on your end?"

**Touch 2 (Day 7) — Value-add:**
"Hi [Name], thought you might find this useful given [their context]: [relevant resource/update]. No response needed — just wanted to share!"

**Touch 3 (Day 14) — Direct + incentive:**
"Hi [Name], we've made some updates since we last spoke that I think could really help with [their goal] — would love to share more if you're open to a quick chat. [Optional: small incentive/offer]"

**Touch 4 (Day 21) — Graceful close:**
"Hi [Name], I'll go ahead and pause outreach for now so I'm not cluttering your inbox — but the door's always open. Wishing you the best, and reach out anytime if [solution] becomes relevant again!"

## SECTION 4 — LEAD SCORING QUICK-RUBRIC
Score each lead 1-5 on each factor, prioritize outreach by total score:
- **Need clarity** (do they have a clear problem your solution addresses?)
- **Budget signal** (have they indicated ability/willingness to invest?)
- **Timeline urgency** (are they looking to solve this now or "eventually"?)
- **Engagement level** (are they responding, opening, clicking — or going quiet?)
- **Decision authority** (are they the decision-maker, or do they need to convince someone else?)

Total 20-25 = prioritize immediately | 12-19 = active nurture | below 12 = long-term nurture list

## 6 RULES FOR CRM SYSTEMS THAT ACTUALLY GET USED
1. Log every interaction immediately — memory fades, the CRM shouldn't have gaps
2. Tag and segment from day one — an unsegmented list is just a graveyard of contacts
3. Set follow-up reminders for EVERY open conversation — nothing should rely on "I'll remember"
4. Review your pipeline weekly — stalled deals need attention before they go cold permanently
5. Track WHY deals are won or lost — patterns here are pure gold for improving your approach
6. Treat existing customers as your highest-value pipeline — retention and referrals cost far less than new acquisition
