## SECTION 1 — COLD OUTREACH (first contact, no prior relationship)

**1. The Problem-Solution Opener**
Subject: Quick question about [their challenge area]
> Hi [Name], noticed [specific observation about their business/role]. Most [their role/industry] I talk to struggle with [specific problem]. We built [solution] to fix exactly that — [one-line proof/result]. Worth a 15-min look?

**2. The Mutual Connection Angle**
Subject: [Mutual contact] suggested I reach out
> Hi [Name], [Mutual contact] mentioned you're working on [project/goal]. I help [type of business] with [specific outcome] — thought it might be relevant. Open to a quick chat?

**3. The Direct Value Offer**
Subject: Free [audit/resource] for [Company]
> Hi [Name], I put together a quick [audit/teardown/resource] for [Company] — found [specific insight]. Want me to send it over? No strings attached.

**4. The Curiosity Hook**
Subject: This took us from [before metric] to [after metric]
> Hi [Name], we recently helped a [similar company] go from [before] to [after] in [timeframe]. Happy to share exactly how if useful for [Company] too.

**5. The Compliment + Pivot**
Subject: Loved what you're doing with [specific thing]
> Hi [Name], saw [specific thing they did] — really well done. It made me think [Company] might also benefit from [your solution] for [specific reason]. Worth exploring?

---

## SECTION 2 — FOLLOW-UPS (after no response)

**6. The Gentle Bump**
Subject: Re: [original subject]
> Hi [Name], following up on my note below — totally understand if timing isn't right. Should I check back in a few months, or is this not relevant at all?

**7. The New Angle**
Subject: One more thought on [topic]
> Hi [Name], wanted to add — we also help with [different angle/benefit]. If [original pitch] wasn't the fit, maybe this is closer to what you need?

**8. The Social Proof Bump**
Subject: [Similar company] just started using this
> Hi [Name], quick update — [similar company/peer] just started working with us on [outcome]. Thought of [Company] given [reason]. Still worth a look?

**9. The Breakup Email**
Subject: Should I close your file?
> Hi [Name], haven't heard back, so I'll assume now isn't the right time. I'll stop following up — but the door's always open if [problem] becomes a priority. Wishing you the best either way.

**10. The Resource Drop**
Subject: Thought this might help regardless
> Hi [Name], even if [original pitch] isn't a fit right now, here's [free resource/guide] that might help with [their challenge]. No follow-up needed — just wanted to share.

## SECTION 3 — WARM/REFERRAL OUTREACH

**11. The Referral Request**
Subject: Quick favor?
> Hi [Name], really enjoyed working with you on [past project]. Do you know anyone else in [industry/role] who might benefit from [your service]? Happy to return the favor anytime.

**12. The Re-engagement**
Subject: It's been a while!
> Hi [Name], realized it's been [time] since we last connected. We've since [new development/offering] — thought you might find it useful given [their context]. Catch up soon?

**13. The Case Study Share**
Subject: How [Client] achieved [result]
> Hi [Name], just published a case study on how [Client] achieved [specific result] using [solution]. Given your work in [their space], thought you'd find it relevant. Link: [URL]

## BONUS — 10 RULES FOR HIGHER REPLY RATES
1. Keep it under 100 words — busy people skim
2. Lead with THEM, not you (their problem/goal, not your features)
3. One clear CTA per email — don't ask for 3 things
4. Personalize the first line — generic openers get deleted
5. Send Tue-Thu, mid-morning — best open-rate windows
6. Follow up at LEAST 3x — most replies come after follow-up #2-4
7. Use a real name + real reply-to — no "noreply@"
8. Avoid spam trigger words: "free," "guarantee," "act now," excessive caps/exclamation
9. Mobile-test every email — most opens happen on phone
10. Track everything — what gets opens vs replies tells you what to scale
