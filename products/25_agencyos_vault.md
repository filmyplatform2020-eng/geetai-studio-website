## SECTION 3 — PRICING & POSITIONING FRAMEWORK

**3 pricing models to choose from:**
- **Project-based**: Fixed price for defined deliverables — best for one-time needs (listings, audits, setup work)
- **Retainer**: Monthly recurring fee for ongoing delivery — best for content, outreach, management services (THIS is where recurring revenue comes from — prioritize moving clients here)
- **Hybrid**: Setup fee + ongoing retainer — best for system-build services (CRM setup, automation builds)

**Positioning rule:** Price based on the OUTCOME/value delivered to the client, not the time it takes you. A lead-gen system that brings in ₹50,000/month in new business is worth far more than "2 hours of writing," even if that's all it takes you with the right tools.

## SECTION 4 — CLIENT ACQUISITION SEQUENCE (your first 10 clients)

1. **Warm network first** — message 20 people you know who run businesses: "I'm offering [service] — would you (or someone you know) want a free sample/audit?"
2. **Free audit/sample as the door-opener** — use your vaults to quickly produce something valuable (a sample listing rewrite, 5 ad copy variants, a mini content calendar) — gives them a taste with zero risk
3. **Local business outreach** — target businesses in your area with a clear, specific opportunity you noticed (use Cold Email/Outreach Vault templates)
4. **Marketplace presence** — list your packaged services on Fiverr/Upwork using the Marketplace Profile Vault structure
5. **Referral system from day one** — every satisfied client gets asked (politely, per the CRM Vault scripts) for an introduction

## SECTION 5 — DELIVERY SYSTEM (how to actually fulfill without burning out)

**The "vault + customize" workflow:**
1. Start from the relevant GeetAI vault template (don't write from scratch — ever)
2. Customize with the client's specific details, voice, and context
3. Review against the "rules" checklist in that vault before delivering
4. Deliver with a brief explanation of WHY each piece works (builds trust, justifies your pricing)

**Time-management rule:** Batch similar work — all content for the week in one sitting, all outreach messages in another. Context-switching is what burns solo operators out, not workload.

## SECTION 6 — SCALING FROM SOLO TO SMALL TEAM

**When to bring in help (signals):**
- You're turning down work because you're at capacity
- You're spending more time on delivery than on acquiring new clients
- You have repeatable processes that someone else could follow with documentation

**First hire priorities (in order):** 1) A generalist VA to handle scheduling/admin/light delivery support, 2) A specialist in your highest-demand service category, 3) A client-success person to manage retainer relationships (frees you to focus on growth)

## 8 RULES FOR BUILDING AN AGENCY THAT ACTUALLY SCALES
1. Sell outcomes, not hours — your pricing should reflect value delivered, not time spent
2. Get clients onto retainers as fast as possible — one-time projects don't build a business, recurring revenue does
3. Systematize everything from day one — what you do manually today should become a template/process by next month
4. Pick 2-3 service categories and go deep — generalist agencies struggle to stand out; specialists command premium prices
5. Always deliver slightly more than promised — the fastest way to build a referral engine
6. Track your numbers — know your cost-per-client-acquired, retention rate, and average client value
7. Don't let delivery work crowd out acquisition — always be doing both, even when you're "busy"
8. Document as you grow — today's "how I do it in my head" is tomorrow's training manual for your first hire
