## SECTION 3 — REVIEW GENERATION SYSTEM (templates)

**In-person/post-service ask (3):**
21. "Hi [Name], so glad we could help with [service]! If you have a moment, a quick Google review would mean a lot to us — here's the link: [link]. Thank you!"
22. "Thanks for visiting us today! If you enjoyed your experience, we'd really appreciate a quick review — it helps other folks in [area] find us too: [link]"

**Follow-up message (after a few days) (2):**
23. "Hi [Name], hope you're enjoying [product/service]! If you have 30 seconds, we'd be grateful for a quick review: [link]. Thanks for supporting local business!"
24. "Hi [Name], quick favor — if [product/service] worked well for you, a short review would help us a lot: [link]. We really appreciate you!"

**Responding to reviews (templates by type):**
- Positive: "Thank you so much, [Name]! We're thrilled [specific thing they mentioned] worked out well for you — looking forward to seeing you again soon!"
- Neutral/mixed: "Thanks for the honest feedback, [Name] — we'd love to make your next experience even better. Please reach out to us directly at [contact] so we can address [their concern]."
- Negative: "We're sorry to hear about your experience, [Name]. This isn't the standard we hold ourselves to — please contact us at [contact] so we can make this right."

## 6 RULES FOR LOCAL BUSINESS MARKETING THAT ACTUALLY DRIVES FOOT TRAFFIC
1. Be hyper-specific to your area — "best [category] in [neighborhood]" beats generic claims every time
2. Make reviews a SYSTEM, not an afterthought — ask consistently, right after a positive experience
3. Respond to every review, good or bad — it shows you're active and care, to everyone reading later
4. Post regularly on Google Business — active profiles get more visibility and trust
5. Build real local relationships — community partnerships compound over time in ways ads don't
6. Track what actually brings people in — and double down on those specific channels/efforts
