## TONE-MATCHING GUIDE
| Customer state | Your tone | Avoid |
|---|---|---|
| Curious/neutral | Friendly, informative | Over-formality |
| Confused | Patient, step-by-step | Jargon, assumptions |
| Frustrated | Calm, validating, action-focused | Defensiveness, over-apologizing without action |
| Angry | Extra calm, ownership-first, solution-fast | Matching their energy, scripts that feel canned |
| Happy/grateful | Warm, personal, light | Generic "glad we could help" without specifics |

## 7 RULES FOR SUPPORT REPLIES THAT BUILD LOYALTY
1. Acknowledge the feeling before addressing the facts — "I understand how frustrating that is" goes a long way
2. Never say "that's not possible" without offering an alternative
3. Take ownership even when it's not "your fault" — customers don't care about internal blame, they care about resolution
4. Be specific about next steps and timelines — vague promises erode trust faster than bad news delivered clearly
5. Follow up proactively — don't make customers chase you for updates
6. Personalize every reply — generic copy-paste responses are easy to spot and feel dismissive
7. End on a warm note — the last line of an interaction shapes how the whole thing is remembered
