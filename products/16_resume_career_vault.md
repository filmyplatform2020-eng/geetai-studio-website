## SECTION 1 — THE RESUME BULLET FORMULA (ATS + human-friendly)

**Structure: [Action verb] + [What you did] + [Measurable result/impact]**

"Increased [metric] by [X%/amount] through [specific action/approach]"
"Led [project/initiative] resulting in [specific outcome]"
"Streamlined [process] — reducing [time/cost/effort] by [measurable amount]"
"Managed [scope — team size/budget/projects] while achieving [result]"

**20 strong action verbs by category:**
- Leadership: Led, Directed, Coordinated, Spearheaded, Championed
- Achievement: Achieved, Delivered, Exceeded, Generated, Secured
- Improvement: Streamlined, Optimized, Transformed, Revamped, Enhanced
- Analysis: Analyzed, Identified, Evaluated, Diagnosed, Assessed

## SECTION 4 — LINKEDIN PROFILE OPTIMIZATION FRAMEWORK

1. **Headline** — Role + specialty + value, not just job title ("[Role] specializing in [specific area] | Helping [audience] [achieve outcome]")
2. **About section** — Open with what drives you professionally, follow with 2-3 concrete achievements, close with what you're looking for/open to
3. **Experience descriptions** — Use the same bullet formula as your resume — outcome-focused, not task-listed
4. **Skills section** — Prioritize the specific skills your target roles search for (research job postings to find these)
5. **Featured section** — Showcase your best work samples, articles, or recommendations prominently

**About section template:**
"[What drives you professionally — one authentic line]. Over [timeframe], I've [specific achievement 1] and [specific achievement 2]. I'm especially energized by [specific type of work/problem you enjoy]. Currently exploring opportunities in [target area] — always happy to connect with [type of people you want to meet]."

## SECTION 5 — INTERVIEW PREP QUICK-FRAMEWORK (STAR method)
For behavioral questions, structure answers as:
- **Situation**: Brief context — what was happening?
- **Task**: What were you responsible for?
- **Action**: What specifically did YOU do? (most important part — be specific)
- **Result**: What happened? Use numbers/outcomes where possible.

---

## 6 RULES FOR RESUMES AND PROFILES THAT GET INTERVIEWS
1. Every bullet should answer "so what?" — if it doesn't show impact, rewrite it
2. Use numbers wherever possible — "improved efficiency" is weak; "improved efficiency by 30%" is strong
3. Tailor to each role — generic resumes get generic (or no) responses
4. Match keywords from the job posting — ATS systems scan for them, and so do hiring managers
5. Keep it scannable — hiring managers spend seconds, not minutes, on a first pass
6. Show growth, not just duties — career progression matters more than task lists
