## SECTION 1 — THE COURSE STRUCTURE FRAMEWORK (outcome-first design)

**Step 1 — Define the transformation:** "By the end of this course, [student] will be able to [specific, concrete outcome]" — every module should map back to this one sentence.

**Step 2 — Reverse-engineer modules from the outcome:**
- Module 1: Foundation — what they need to understand before anything else
- Module 2-4: Core skill-building — broken into the smallest logical steps
- Module 5: Application — putting it together in a real scenario
- Module 6: Next steps — how to keep growing after the course ends

**Step 3 — Each module = 3-5 lessons, each lesson = ONE clear idea**
(Cognitive load rule: cramming multiple ideas into one lesson is the #1 reason students disengage)

## SECTION 2 — 15 LESSON SCRIPT FRAMEWORKS

**Framework 1 — The Concept Lesson**
"Here's what we're covering today: [concept]. Why it matters: [practical relevance]. Here's how it works: [explanation broken into 2-3 simple parts]. Here's an example: [concrete scenario]. Quick recap: [one-line summary]."

**Framework 2 — The Skill-Building Lesson**
"By the end of this lesson, you'll be able to [specific mini-outcome]. Here's how: [Step 1 — what+why] → [Step 2] → [Step 3]. Now it's your turn: [practice prompt]."

**Framework 3 — The Case Study Lesson**
"Let's look at a real example: [situation]. Here's what they did: [actions taken]. Here's what happened: [results]. Here's what you can take from this: [extractable lesson]."

**Framework 4 — The Myth-Busting Lesson**
"A lot of people believe [common misconception]. Here's why that's incomplete: [explanation]. Here's what's actually true: [correct understanding]. Here's how this changes your approach: [practical implication]."

**Framework 5 — The Walkthrough/Demo Lesson**
"I'm going to show you exactly how this works, step by step: [narrate each step as you demonstrate]. Notice [key detail to pay attention to]. Now you try it with [practice scenario]."

## SECTION 3 — COURSE LAUNCH EMAIL SEQUENCE (8-email framework)

**Email 1 — The Problem (pre-launch):** "Have you ever struggled with [problem]? You're not alone — here's why this is so common, and what most people get wrong about solving it."

**Email 2 — The Possibility (pre-launch):** "Here's what's possible when you approach [problem] differently — [brief story/example of transformation]. I've put together something that walks you through exactly how."

**Email 3 — The Announcement (launch day):** "It's here — [Course name] is officially open! Here's exactly what's inside and who it's for: [brief overview + enrollment link]."

**Email 4 — The Curriculum Walkthrough:** "Here's exactly what you'll learn, module by module: [brief outline]. By the end, you'll be able to [outcome]."

**Email 5 — The Social Proof/Story:** "[Testimonial/case study/personal story illustrating the transformation this course delivers]."

**Email 6 — The Objection-Handler:** "Thinking 'I don't have time' or 'I'm not sure this is for me'? Here's how [course] is designed to work around exactly those concerns: [specific reassurance]."

**Email 7 — The Urgency/Deadline Reminder:** "Enrollment closes [date/time] — here's a quick recap of what's included, and why now is the right time to start: [recap + link]."

**Email 8 — The Last-Call:** "Final hours to enroll in [course name]. If you've been on the fence, here's the one thing to consider: [reframe their hesitation as the cost of waiting]. [Link]"

## 6 RULES FOR COURSES THAT GET FINISHED (NOT ABANDONED)
1. One clear outcome per course — vague promises ("learn everything about X") create unfocused content and disengaged students
2. One idea per lesson — overloaded lessons are the #1 reason students stop midway
3. Build in quick wins early — early progress keeps motivation high through the harder middle sections
4. Use real examples over abstract theory — concrete beats conceptual every time
5. Make it easy to take action — every lesson should end with something the student can DO, not just know
6. Follow up during the course, not just at launch — engaged students finish; ignored students drop off
